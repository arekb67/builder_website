My recommendation: Palette 1 (Ink & Cloud)

It's the crispest of the three — the deep navy gives headings real punch against the warm white, and the muted brass accent is warm without being heavy. It reads as "sophisticated London" rather than "cosy country" or "neutral safe".

The navy also solves the "crispness" issue you raised — it provides much stronger contrast than charcoal or mushroom, which makes the page feel sharp and defined. And the brass accent works beautifully with both warm and cool photography, so it'll pair well with whatever AI images we generate.

Palette 2 (Sage) is the freshest and most on-trend, but sage green in a builder's brand might feel more "wellness" than "construction". Palette 3 (Mushroom) is the safest and most timeless, but it's the least crisp — it's warm and refined but lacks the bite you asked for.
